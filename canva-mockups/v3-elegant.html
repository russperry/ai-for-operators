01 — Why now

Canva is a license. Brand operations is a discipline.

For a 300-person field team, a multi-campus university system, or a multi-brand portfolio, "self-serve design" without operational infrastructure is a slow erosion of brand equity.

i.

The brand book is documentation. The Canva account is reality.

Without locked templates and governed assets, every regional manager becomes a designer. Within ninety days the brand has drifted in fourteen directions and no one is auditing it.

ii.

Senior designers become a help desk for the rest of the company.

Strategic creative work is displaced by template approvals and rebuild requests. The hires you made for vision are now rebuilding sell sheets at scale.

iii.

Twelve years of brand assets remain in legacy formats.

Migration of a thousand-file library is a six-month internal initiative no one volunteers to own. So Canva adoption stalls at the marketing department and never reaches the field.

iv.

Agency invoices continue. The deployment was supposed to end them.

Canva was approved on the case for cost displacement. Eighteen months later, the agency line item is unchanged, and the design budget has new fees on top of it.
